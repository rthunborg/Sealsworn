# Epic 12 — Auto-GDS retro notes

## Story 12-2-class-loadout-and-winnable-hands-on-fights
- [Phase 3 — create-story] Winnability crux: all three class kits are 18 HP and the existing focus-fire `LiveCombatResolver` provably dies at 18 HP on a full live walk (the 11.3 lesson) — AC2 hard-forces a strengthened LoS-aware reference driver, not a trivial HP swap.
- [Phase 3 — create-story] AC4 interaction: threading the warrior `shield` support engages a `shield_block` roll on the `combat` stream in `AttackCommand` — an INTENTIONAL, seeded fixture re-pin on the class path only; neutral default/auto-resolve/generator paths must stay byte-identical. Most likely site for a reviewer to mistake an intentional fingerprint change for a regression.
- [Phase 5 — dev-story] The dominant 18-HP death is ash-seer detonations on a STATIONARY hero, not melee — `marked_tile_detonated` only hits if the hero ends a turn on the marked cell, so detonation-dodge is THE mechanic making all three classes winnable at 18 HP. The melee warrior is the winnability bottleneck; the approved seed batch [4242, 8080, 6006, 2048, 512] is curated — heavy multi-melee seeds are NOT warrior-winnable at 18 HP and would correctly fail loud.
- [Phase 5 — dev-story] Support-slot modeling gap: the warrior's shield (threaded as `defender_support`) engages the pinned `shield_block` roll but the AttackCommand seam has no path for a hero's shield to reduce INCOMING enemy damage — the shield slightly slows the warrior's own kills. Winnability comes from positioning, not the shield; the shield is a distinctness/`combat`-draw signal. A future combat story could add a hero-defense support seam.
