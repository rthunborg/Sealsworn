## 4. What Was Challenging / Growth Areas

**Amelia (Developer):** The genuine engineering subtlety of the epic was the FR58 Darkness finding (10-3 → 10-8), and it was not a cosmetic fix. 7.6's `DarknessFairnessQuery` suite only ever exercised Small (all-FLOOR) seeds, so Medium-recipe baked hazards under Darkness (seeds 4004/5005) went undetected until the 10-3 batch ran the whole recipe×affinity matrix — and 10-3's own Dev Notes carried the false premise "v0 boards are all-FLOOR" (true only for Small). The strengthening itself is subtle: the entrance-only static-LoS predicate false-POSITIVE-failed boards that are actually fair because the hero necessarily SEES the hazard before contact under stepwise movement at the reduced radius. Getting that right meant formalizing "seen-before-contact" and DELIBERATELY updating four stale static-from-entrance expectations that a predicate-contract change fans out to — two of which (the live-gate violation board in `test_live_affinity_flow.gd` and 10.2's honest-sample assertion block) weren't named in the epics.md ACs and were only found by a full-codebase grep sweep. The lesson: when you change a validator/predicate contract, grep-sweep ALL tests/tools/ledgers for baked expectations before scoping the ACs.

**Charlie (Senior Dev / Reviewer):** The honest limitation of a gate epic is that its most load-bearing evidence lives OUTSIDE the suite. 10-6's verdict rests on ten AC1 loop steps, and three of them carry a qualifier the suite can't fully close: "view summary" is present-but-THIN (`RunEndProfileBridge` builds `RunSummary.build(run, [])`, so `outcome_or_cause` stays blank and the summary must key the outcome off `phase`), and "collect rewards" + "make passive choices" are PRESENT and integration-proven but CALLER-DRIVEN — not yet wired into the live run-flow HUD/scene (per `test_loot_passive_build_smoke_run.gd`'s own header). None of these is a MISSING step, so none blocks AC1 — but a reviewer has to be disciplined about the difference between "present + integration-proven" and "present + live-HUD-wired," and record it as a limitation with an owner rather than let the gate's green suite imply more than it proves.

**Dana (QA Engineer):** Two things needed careful, non-obvious verification. First, the stderr catalog: 10-8's retro note flagged that int64-overflow might emit ×1 on some build states vs the catalog's ×2 — and on the 10-6 gate build it emits ×2 (both `test_domain_event.gd` and `test_manual_seed_loader.gd` fire), so the catalog is exact on the gate head, but this is a benign catalog-vs-build reconcile item that must NEVER be "fixed" by changing a test. Second, the reference-driver proof harness runs ~57 s in isolation (a third of the full-suite wall-clock) because `_best_end_cell` → `_relocate_scratch` does a full `board.to_snapshot()`→`try_from_snapshot()` round-trip per candidate cell, amplified by 10-4's Medium/Scorched catalog extension. It's not a correctness defect (the harness is excluded from every export and the suite is green) and not blocking — but it's a real developer-experience cost recorded against an owner, and its byte-determinism proof still covers only the original Small catalog, not the added Medium/Scorched entries (a coverage-completeness gap, though determinism holds in fact).

**Cloud (Game Architect):** The readiness verdict is honest precisely because it is `READY_WITH_GATES`, not `READY` — and it's worth being clear-eyed that the gated items are the ones a headless agent structurally CANNOT discharge. No physical low/mid/high-tier device pass (G1-G6), no on-device/windowed render-frame FPS profiler (device-tiers §6 G4 — a headless run has no render frame loop), no real touch-to-glass latency, no physical battery/thermal read, and no producible iOS binary (G7 — `preset.2` is a `runnable=false` scaffold needing macOS + Xcode). The desktop/headless measurements pass with enormous margin and are a strong lower bound, but the felt on-device read is a genuine pre-ship follow-up. Shipping the FINAL build (as opposed to preserving the v0 candidate) is gated on that physical-device lab pass — and that's the correct architecture posture, not a shortfall of the epic.

**Sally (UX Designer):** Two surfaces are audited on paper, not on glass, because the glass doesn't exist yet. 3. **When you change a validator/predicate contract, grep-sweep ALL tests/tools/ledgers for baked expectations before scoping the ACs.** 10-8's moving-LoS strengthening fanned out to four stale static-from-entrance expectations, two of which weren't named in the epics.md ACs and were only found by a full-codebase sweep. 4. **Strengthening a fairness VALIDATOR that reads generator output moves no fingerprint — keep the deliberate-drift discipline anyway.** 10-8 flipped two Medium seeds FAIL→PASS because the predicate got more correct; the query isn't fingerprinted, so no seed re-pinned, but the finding-presence expectations + fairness-verdict tests were updated IN PLACE with a real FAIL retained as a guard, and a new moving-LoS proof added. A regression baseline that flips must flip on PURPOSE, in a test, with a guard.
5. **Alternate-model review passes catch real errors a same-model pass certifies.** 10-7's AC2 event map was blessed as correct by the primary Round-1 reviewer, yet mis-attributed the hero-death cue to a non-existent `RUN_COMPLETED` "failed" outcome instead of `RUN_FAILED` — caught only by the alternate-model Round-2 pass. Model diversity is worth the extra round even on a doc a green pass already approved.
6. **A gate's honesty depends on distinguishing "present + integration-proven" from "present + live-HUD-wired," and "measured headless" from "felt on hardware."** 10-6's ten loop steps are all present, but three are qualified (thin summary; caller-driven reward/passive) and the physical-device dimensions are gaps — recording those precisely is what makes `READY_WITH_GATES` a real verdict rather than a green rubber stamp.
7. **A validation epic is only as real as the thing it validates — surface the sequencing dependency at plan time.** Epic 10's gates assumed a playable-then-winnable loop that wasn't in its list; it took three mid-epic inserts (Epic 11, Epic 12, 10-8) to reconcile. The retro chain caught it (Epic 9 → 11 → 12 retros), which is the system working, but the dependency between a readiness epic and the features it gates belongs in the plan, not the retro.
8. **The atomic finalize that seven retros re-escalated finally took — because the whole finalize (story-status flip + context refresh + resolved-work archive) landed before the retro ran, not after.** The streak broke when the tracking-file half was done on disk AND both capstone story fields read `done` at retro time. Keep the finalize atomic; don't let the epic-level flip drift back into a lagging story field.
