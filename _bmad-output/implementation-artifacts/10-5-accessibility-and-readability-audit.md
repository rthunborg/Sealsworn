## Story

As a player,
I want critical tactical information to remain accessible in the MVP build,
so that readability problems are caught before broader testing.

## Story Type & Scope Boundary (READ FIRST)

**This is a READINESS / AUDIT story — the direct sibling of Story 10.1 (device-tiers readiness doc), Story 10.4 (playtest-comprehension checklist), and Story 11.1 (the run-flow UX appendix). It is NOT a gameplay-feature story and NOT a UI-rework story.** The primary deliverable is a **systematic accessibility & readability AUDIT report (a markdown planning artifact)** authored under `_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/`, that audits the ALREADY-SHIPPED accessibility contract across the full MVP screen roster (tactical HUD + previews + inspect + hazards/affinities/telegraphs + passive modal + route map + outpost + run summary + reveal beats + settings + save/resume recovery), records every finding with **screen + state + issue**, and hands unresolved items to the 10.6 readiness gate. It discharges the readability-validation half of the Epic-10 MVP-readiness mandate (NFR7 phone readability, NFR8 scalable text, NFR9 colorblind-safe / no color-only meaning) so the 10.6 gate consumes a real, structured audit rather than guesswork.

- **The single required deliverable is the AUDIT REPORT doc.** Everything the ACs enumerate (the color-independence check, the scalable-text check, the non-overlap check, the no-color-only check, the phone portrait/landscape reachability check, the orientation-invariance check, the audio-off equivalence check) is CONTENT of that doc: for EACH audited surface, state what was checked, the pass/fail read, and record any failure with **screen + state + issue**. Author it, cite the surface/contract each check rests on, and record honest availability gaps for anything a headless agent cannot perform (e.g. a human-eyes contrast-ratio measurement on a physical phone).
- **The accessibility CONTRACT already exists and is deeply tested — the audit VERIFIES it, it does NOT rebuild it.** Story 2.6 shipped the codified color-independence contract (`TacticalAccessibilityModel._CUE_CATALOG` — every critical cue carries ≥1 non-color channel from `shape/icon/label/pattern/text`; `TacticalTextScale` clamps text to `[0.85, 2.0]`), exhaustively tested by `godot/tests/unit/ui/test_tactical_accessibility_cues.gd` (16 audit methods). Story 2.9 shipped the persisted preferences (`SettingsSnapshot.colorblind_safe / high_contrast / text_scale`). Story 11.1's UX appendix §0.5 + §14 codified that EVERY run-flow screen inherits that same contract, and Epic 11 built the scenes (route_map / outpost / run_end / save_recovery / gameplay_shell / hero_select `.tscn`). **The audit's job is to confirm the contract is complete and honored across the WHOLE roster and flag the gaps — NOT to re-derive the cue catalog, re-implement a sanitizer, or re-write a presenter.** [Source: `godot/scripts/ui/view_models/tactical_accessibility_model.gd`; `ux-appendix-run-flow.md` §0.5/§14; `project-context.md` § Presentation, View-Model & Accessibility Rules]
- **This is NOT a domain/tactical/save/RNG/content/generator/scene story.** Do NOT change any gameplay command, event, RNG stream, `RunSnapshot`/`ProfileSnapshot`/`SettingsSnapshot` schema, save key, generator/route/finale fingerprint, view model, content definition, presenter, or `.tscn`. ## Acceptance Criteria

Sourced verbatim from `epics.md` (Epic 10, Story 10.5). Three AC groups (Given/When/Then + And).

1. **The accessibility audit across the full surface (AC1).** GIVEN **tactical HUD, previews, hazards, affinities, telegraphs, passive modal, route map, outpost, and run summary** are available, WHEN accessibility audit runs, THEN critical information is checked for **colorblind-safe communication, scalable text, non-overlap, and no color-only meaning**, AND **failures are recorded with screen, state, and issue**.

2. **Phone portrait/landscape reachability + orientation invariance (AC2).** GIVEN **phone portrait and landscape viewports** are tested, WHEN **core combat and reward flows** are exercised, THEN **primary actions remain reachable and readable**, AND **orientation changes do not alter tactical rules**.

3. **Audio-off equivalence (AC3).** GIVEN **audio is muted or unavailable**, WHEN **preview, confirm, warning, damage, and reward feedback** occur, THEN **visual or textual equivalents communicate critical meaning**, AND **no required information is audio-only**.

### AC Verification (how "done" is checked)

- **AC1** — the audit doc has a per-surface section covering EVERY named surface (tactical HUD, previews, hazards, affinities, telegraphs, passive modal, route map, outpost, run summary — and, for roster-completeness, inspect, hero select, first-death reveal, first-victory reveal, manual-seed warning, settings, save/resume recovery per appendix §0.7). For EACH surface it records the four checks — **colorblind-safe communication, scalable text, non-overlap, no color-only meaning** — each with a concrete pass/fail read and the CONTRACT it rests on (the cue-catalog channel set / the `TacticalTextScale` clamp / the presenter's non-color mapping / the appendix §14.2 rule). Every FINDING (a fail or a gap) is recorded with **screen + state + issue** in a consolidated findings table. Missing a named surface, missing any of the four checks, or a finding lacking screen/state/issue = AC1 not met.
- **AC2** — the doc has a section stating the phone-portrait + phone-landscape reachability + readability read (primary actions ≥44×44 and reachable on the compact profiles per §14.1; the board/primary region stays dominant), AND the orientation-invariance statement backed by the existing rule-invariance evidence (`test_run_flow_layout_invariance.gd` proves the contract is byte-identical across profile changes and text-scale never alters rules; `TacticalLayoutProfile` owns the four-profile plan incl. the invalid-viewport fallback). A human-eyes phone-hardware pass (physical device readability, real thumb-reach) is recorded as an availability gap → owning action (the physical-device pass owned by the 10.6 gate, intersecting the same G1–G7 device constraint 10.1 recorded). Missing the reachability read, the orientation-invariance statement, or the gap note = AC2 not met.
- **AC3** — the doc has a section stating that with audio muted/unavailable, the preview/confirm/warning/damage/reward feedback keeps a visual/textual equivalent, backed by the contract fact (`TacticalAccessibilityModel` feedback cues keep `visual_available: true` independent of `audio_available`; the two feedback cues declare parallel *optional* audio cue ids that never carry sole meaning; audio is a 0-file placeholder track in v0 so nothing is audio-only today), AND the explicit "no required information is audio-only" conclusion + a flag for any surface that would violate it (none in v0). Missing the equivalence statement, the contract backing, or the no-audio-only conclusion = AC3 not met.
- **AC-support (headless facts the audit rests on)** — if a test is added, it PROVES the accessibility facts a headless agent CAN prove across the FULL roster (not just the tactical HUD the 2.6 test already covers): (a) every cue id emitted by the run-flow affinity/Darkness read models (`LiveAffinityReadModel.cue_ids`, `DarknessReadView.cue_ids`) is registered in `TacticalAccessibilityModel` with a non-color channel (extending the 2.6 audit to the run-flow roster); and/or (b) a consolidated assertion that the roster's registered critical cues each carry a non-color channel + the text-scale clamp holds. The test asserts on readiness FACTS, NOT on new gameplay behavior; the full suite stays green (a NEW passing `test_*.gd`, no existing pin moved).

## Dev Notes

### What this story is (and is not)

Epics 1-9 shipped a complete headless deterministic domain; Story 2.6 shipped the CODIFIED color-independence + scalable-text accessibility contract (the tactical-HUD cue catalog, deeply tested); Story 2.9 shipped the persisted accessibility preferences; Epic 11 wired the run-flow scenes (route map / outpost / run summary / reveal / save-recovery) and the UX appendix §0.5/§14 that binds every screen to that same contract; Epic 12 wired the interactive tap-loop. What the project has NEVER had is a **single systematic accessibility & readability AUDIT that verifies the contract is complete + honored across the WHOLE MVP screen roster and records the gaps** — the NFR7/NFR8/NFR9 readability half of the Epic-10 readiness mandate. **Story 10.5 is the audit-plus-honest-gaps that closes it**: it audits the shipped contract per-surface, verifies every headless-verifiable fact, and records the human-eyes-on-hardware dimension as an explicit availability gap.

The single most important discipline (mirroring 10.1, 10.4, 11.1): **audit what CAN be audited + verify what CAN be verified now; record honest gaps for the human-eyes-on-hardware rest — do NOT rebuild the accessibility contract, re-implement a sanitizer, re-write a presenter, or silently expand into a UI-rework/scene change.** This is a readiness-audit story; the contract, the view models, and the scenes are untouched.

### The measurement reality (why human-eyes-on-hardware checks are availability gaps, not blockers)

- The ACs are dischargeable WITHOUT a physical-device lab because the accessibility CONTRACT is codified and testable: the audit legitimately produces the complete per-surface audit, the findings table, the phone-profile reachability read (grounded in `TacticalLayoutProfile` + the layout-invariance test), the audio-off equivalence (grounded in the feedback-cue `visual_available` contract), the headless-verifiable roster cue-coverage fact, and an availability-gap ledger for the human-eyes dimension (a real contrast-ratio measurement on a physical phone, real thumb-reach on hardware).
- This is the SAME honesty posture 10.1 used (record the gap against an owner rather than invent the data) and 10.4 used (the observed-session gaps). The gate story (10.6) decides whether a still-open human-eyes accessibility gap is an acceptable documented readiness LIMITATION or must be discharged by a real device pass before MVP-readiness passes. 10.5's job is to make the audit + findings + gaps explicit + dischargeable, not to run the device lab.
- **Do NOT stop and ask a human to run a physical-device accessibility pass as the primary outcome.** The story is completable and valuable without it (the audit + findings + verified contract facts + gap ledger). Only escalate to `needs-human` if a required deliverable is genuinely un-authorable without an external secret/service — which is not the case here.
