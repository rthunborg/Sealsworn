# Story 6.6: Destroy Passive Command and Outcome Distribution

## Story

As a player,
I want to Destroy a passive for safety, resources, refusal, or hidden progress,
so that Destroy is a meaningful alternative to Consume.

## Acceptance Criteria

Source: `_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/epics.md` -> Epic 6 -> Story 6.6 (verbatim AC, with the implementation-grounded detail this story adds). FRs covered: **FR49** (Destroy must provide safety, purification, resources, secrets, refusal, or future-consequence avoidance and must be a meaningful alternative, NOT a dead option), **FR50** (Destroy outcomes must support small immediate benefits, progress/hidden flags, and no-obvious-reward outcomes that avoid corruption/future danger), and **FR82** (epics.md line 397 — the Destroy outcome **70/20/10 distribution** maps to 6.6; this is the SECOND half of the FR82 Consume/Destroy resolution split — Story 6.5 shipped the Consume half). It consumes the FR47 commit-INTENT (`{choice:"destroy", passive_content_id, table_id}`) that Story 6.4's `PassiveRewardCommitFlow.confirm()` produces. Builds DIRECTLY on, and MIRRORS, **Story 6.5's `ConsumePassiveCommand`** (`godot/scripts/core/commands/consume_passive_command.gd`) — the validate-then-mutate run-command idiom, the offer-flip + selected-entry posture, the `_invalid_context(inner)` helper, the new-SYSTEM-event-appended-at-enum-end pattern — and on Story 6.3's `RewardOffer` value object + the `passive` offered-entry shape, Story 6.4's `destroy_text`/`consume_text`/honest-unknown modal fields on `PassiveDefinition`, and the Story-1.4 `RngStreamSet` named-stream draw API. This is a **COMMAND + EVENT + CONTENT-DEFINITION + RUN-STATE-MUTATION story**: it builds the real `DestroyPassiveCommand`, a new validated `DestroyOutcomeTableDefinition` (the 70/20/10 outcome pool), emits a new `passive_destroyed` SYSTEM event, and ROLLS the Destroy outcome through a **named RNG stream** (`rewards`). It does NOT build the per-effect passive OPERATION engine (a later Epic-6 operations story — `scripts/rules/{conditions,operations}` stay EMPTY), the live currency/wallet/heal/cleanse/curse-removal MUTATIONS the Destroy outcomes flavor-reference (Epic 7 risk-economy + Epic 8 meta — v0 Destroy is OUTCOME-RECORD-ONLY), or any `.tscn` modal scene (UI-scene-last; AC5's modal text already ships on `PassiveDefinition` from 6.4).

1. **An unresolved passive offer + a Destroy choice -> `DestroyPassiveCommand` validates the offer, resolves a deterministic Destroy outcome, and emits a passive-destroyed event recording the passive id and outcome category.**
   **Given** a passive reward offer is unresolved (`RunState.pending_reward_offer != null` AND `is_pending()`, carrying a `{category: "passive", content_id: <passive_id>}` offered entry)
   **When** the player chooses Destroy (a caller hands the `{passive_content_id, table_id}` from the FR47 commit-intent to `DestroyPassiveCommand.new(passive_content_id, table_id, sequence_id, ...)`)
   **Then** `DestroyPassiveCommand` VALIDATES the offer (validate-then-mutate, the run-command idiom VERBATIM from `ConsumePassiveCommand`: reject `sequence_id <= 0` FIRST with `invalid_event_sequence_id`; reject a non-`RunState`/structurally-invalid run with `invalid_context`; reject no pending offer with `no_pending_reward_offer`; reject an already-`resolved` offer with `reward_offer_already_resolved`; reject a selected passive id that is NOT a `passive`-category offered entry on the pending offer with `invalid_reward_selection`; reject a passive id that does not resolve through `PassiveRepository.get_passive(id)` with `unknown_passive`) and **RESOLVES A DETERMINISTIC DESTROY OUTCOME** (roll the 70/20/10 outcome category from the validated `DestroyOutcomeTableDefinition` through the run-level `RngStreamSet` `rewards` stream — the assigned named stream; flip the offer to `resolved` + record the selected entry, the `ConsumePassiveCommand`/`ResolveRewardCommand` offer-resolution posture)
   **And** a `passive_destroyed` event records the passive id and outcome category (a NEW `passive_destroyed` SYSTEM `DomainEvent` appended at the enum end after `PASSIVE_CONSUMED`, NEVER renumbering — carrying the lower_snake `passive_id`, the `table_id`, the rolled `outcome_category`, the specific `outcome_id`, AND the draw provenance `roll`/`draw_index` (mirroring `reward_offered`, NOT `passive_consumed`, because Destroy DRAWS RNG)).

2. **MVP Destroy outcomes support small immediate benefits, progress/unlock/hidden flags, and no-obvious-reward outcomes that avoid future danger; outcome rolls use the assigned `rewards` (or `events`) RNG stream.**
   **Given** MVP Destroy outcomes are configured (the `DestroyOutcomeTableDefinition` carries weighted entries across the THREE FR50/GDD outcome categories — `small_immediate_benefit`, `progress_unlock_hidden_flag`, `no_obvious_reward_avoids_danger`)
   **When** outcome selection runs
   **Then** it supports all three categories (the table holds at least one entry per category; the weighted draw can yield any of the three) **And** outcome rolls use the assigned `rewards` RNG stream (the architecture's named-RNG-streams-only rule — `RngStreamSet.STREAM_REWARDS`, the SAME stream the reward DRAW uses; NOT a hand-minted `RandomNumberGenerator`, NOT the global RNG. **DECISION TO RECORD:** the GDD/AC lists "`rewards` OR `events`" — pick `rewards` (the reward/passive-offer resolution stream the offer was already drawn on) and record the choice as a `[Decision]`; consistency with the 6.3 reward-draw stream is the reason.).

3. **The validated MVP Destroy outcome table targets 70 percent small immediate benefit, 20 percent progress/unlock/hidden flag, and 10 percent no obvious reward; any temporary tuning deviation records reason, date, and owner.**
   **Given** the MVP Destroy outcome table is validated
   **When** configured outcome weights are checked
   **Then** the target distribution is 70% small immediate benefit, 20% progress/unlock/hidden flag, 10% no obvious reward that avoids corruption/future danger (`DestroyOutcomeTableDefinition.validate()` REJECTS a table whose per-category weight shares do not sum to the 70/20/10 target — i.e. the summed weight of each category's entries divided by the total weight must equal 70%/20%/10% — UNLESS an explicit, visible `mvp_distribution_exception` marker is set WITH a non-empty `distribution_exception_reason`, mirroring `RewardTableDefinition.mvp_choice_count_exception` + `choice_count_exception_reason` and the 6.1 `*_mvp_deferred` posture; the exception is SURFACED — this validate() check + a tuning note — never a silent reduction) **And** any temporary tuning deviation records the reason, date, and owner in content or tuning notes (the `distribution_exception_reason` string + a line in this story's Completion Notes; the BASELINE MVP table ships AT the exact 70/20/10 target with NO exception marker — author entry weights that land exactly on 70/20/10, e.g. weights 70/20/10 or 7/2/1).

4. **A Destroy outcome that cleanses/heals/grants gold/grants Oath-Shards-placeholder-progress/rerolls-future-rewards/advances-a-hidden-flag records the exact mechanical effect via domain events, and the explanation log communicates the known result.**
   **Given** a Destroy outcome cleanses, heals, grants gold, grants Oath Shards placeholder progress, rerolls future rewards, or advances a hidden flag
   **When** it resolves
   **Then** domain events record the EXACT mechanical effect (the `passive_destroyed` event payload carries the rolled `outcome_category` + the specific `outcome_id` + a stable `outcome_effect` marker describing the deterministic effect — **SCOPE CLARIFICATION (READ): v0 Destroy outcomes are OUTCOME-RECORD-ONLY** — the live currency/WALLET (gold), the healing/cleanse/curse-removal state, the Oath-Shards/mastery/unlock meta-progression, and the future-reward reroll state do NOT exist as domain fields in v0. So a Destroy outcome RECORDS its rolled category + outcome id + effect marker as a deterministic OUTCOME — EXACTLY as Story 6.3's `ResolveRewardCommand` records a GOLD reward as a `reward_resolved` outcome ONLY with no wallet mutation (resolve_reward_command.gd:26-31 `[Decision]`). 5. **The modal communicates what is gained, what is refused/sacrificed, and whether known consequences exist; Destroy does not read like a generic salvage button.**
   **Given** the player compares Consume and Destroy
   **When** the modal presents both choices
   **Then** the text communicates what is gained, what is refused or sacrificed, and whether known consequences exist (this is ALREADY shipped by Story 6.4's `PassiveDefinition` modal fields — `consume_text` (what Consume gives), `destroy_text` (what Destroy gives — safety/purification/resources/refusal), `has_unknown_consequences` + `consequences_text` (the honest-unknown contract), surfaced by `PassiveRewardModalViewModel`. **SCOPE: this AC is satisfied by the EXISTING 6.4 data contract — do NOT re-author the modal fields, do NOT build a `.tscn` modal scene.** 6.6's contribution is that the Destroy CHOICE now has a real resolving command behind it (`DestroyPassiveCommand`), so `destroy_text` is no longer a promise with no command — Destroy is a meaningful alternative with a real 70/20/10 outcome) **And** Destroy does not read like a generic salvage button (the six baseline passives' `destroy_text` already communicates an intentional fiction per 6.4; 6.6 makes Destroy mechanically meaningful via the three-category outcome distribution rather than a flat "salvage".
